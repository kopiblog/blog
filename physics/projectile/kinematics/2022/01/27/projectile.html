Projectile Motion : An Analysis

Aim

A projectile is launched with velocity $v_0$ at an angle θ to the horizontal. We want to investigate and analyze its trajectory and study the effect of changing the launch angle θ. We illustrate it visually for a clearer understanding.

1. Maximum Height

For finding maximum height $h$ reached by the projectile, we consider motion in vertical direction i.e. along y-axis.

initial velocity = $v_0sinθ$
final velocity = $0$
acceleration = $-g$

Using
$\begin{align}v^2 - u^2 = 2as\end{align}$
we get
$\begin{align}0^2 - v_0^2sin^2θ = 2(-g)h\end{align}$
$\begin{align}v_0^2sin^2θ = 2gh\end{align}$

i.e.

$\begin{align}\Large h = \frac{v_0^2sin^2θ}{2g}\end{align}$

2. Time of Flight

For finding maximum height $h$, we again consider motion in vertical direction.

initial velocity = $v_0sinθ$
displacement = $0$
acceleration = $-g$

Using $\begin{align}s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2\end{align}$
we get
$\begin{align}0 = v_0tsinθ - \frac{1}{2}gt^2\end{align}$
$\begin{align}0 = t(v_0sinθ - \frac{1}{2}gt)\end{align}$

This gives
$\begin{align}t = 0\end{align}$
or
$\begin{align}v_0sinθ - \frac{1}{2}gt = 0\end{align}$

Thus, the time of flight is as below :-

$\begin{align}\Large t = \frac{2v_0sinθ}{g}\end{align}$

3. Range

For finding the range $s$ or the distance where the projectile lands on the ground, we consider motion in horizontal direction i.e. along x-axis.

Initial Velocity = $v_0cosθ$

Time of Flight = $\frac{2v_0sinθ}{g}$ (from section 2)

$\begin{align}s = ut\end{align}$

$\begin{align} = \frac{(v_0cosθ)(2v_0sinθ)}{g}\end{align}$

$\begin{align} = \frac{2v_0^2sinθcosθ}{g}\end{align}$

$\begin{align} = \frac{v_0^2sin2θ}{g}\end{align}$

i.e.

$\Large s = \frac{v_0^2sin2θ}{g}$
